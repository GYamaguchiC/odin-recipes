Yakisoba

Description

Yakisoba is a popular Japanese stir-fried noodle dish made with wheat noodles, vegetables like cabbage and carrots, and thinly sliced meat, all tossed in a savory-sweet sauce. Despite its name meaning “fried soba,” it’s usually made with Chinese-style wheat noodles rather than buckwheat soba. It’s quick, delicious, and perfect for lunch or dinner!

Ingredients

  • 2 packs of yakisoba noodle (pre-cooked or fresh)
  • 150g thinly sliced pork or chicken
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1/2 carrot, jilienned
  • 1 cup chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 table spoons yakisoba sauce
  • 1 table spoon vegetable oil
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • pickled ginger
  • Aonori

Steps

  1. Prepare the noodles by placing them in warm water or microwaving for 30 seconds
  2. Add the oil to the pan and heat it
  3. Cook the meat till browned
  4. Stir-fry the vegetables for 2-3 minutes
  5. Add the noodles
  6. Add the sauce
  7. Adjust salt and pepper
  8. Serve
